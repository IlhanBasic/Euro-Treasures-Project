Most Famous Art

Mona Lisa

"Mona Lisa" by Leonardo da Vinci is a renowned Renaissance masterpiece portraying Lisa del Giocondo, displayed at the Louvre Museum in Paris.

The Night Watch

"The Night Watch" (Dutch: De Nachtwacht) is a famous 1642 painting by Rembrandt van Rijn, displayed at the Rijksmuseum. It depicts musketeers from the Dutch Golden Age, likely members of Kloveniersdoelen.

The school of Athens

"The School of Athens" by Raphael is a renowned Renaissance artwork in the Vatican's Raphael Rooms. It portrays various philosophers and represents key societal principles: reason, goodness, beauty, and justice.

The Latest Significant Galleries

Alfonso Artiaco

Alfonso Artiaco, aged 22, debuted his Pozzuoli gallery in 1986, exhibiting collections by acclaimed artists like Boetti, Warhol, and others.

Christophe Guye Galerie

Established in 2006, the gallery showcases artists blending photography into contemporary art, hosting multiple exhibitions and collaborating with institutions and collectors.

Galleri Magnus Karlsson

Founded in 1990, Galleri Magnus Karlsson initially in Västerås, gained acclaim as Sweden's captivating contemporary art space, relocating to Stockholm in 1997.

Mobius Gallery

Since 2015, Mobius Gallery champions Eastern European art, fostering dialogue and collaboration through exhibitions, talks, and publications, becoming a pivotal Romanian art hub.

Walter Storms Galerie

Walter Storms Gallery, established in 1977 by Walter Storms, stands as a premier international art institution. Renowned for its representation of influential ZERO artists pivotal in post-war art, it specializes in contemporary, concrete, and conceptual art.

Dorotheum

Established in 1707 by Emperor Joseph I, Dorotheum is among the oldest auction houses worldwide, embodying centuries of Viennese history in its magnificent Palais Dorotheum.

Our mission

EuroTreasure's mission is to spotlight European museums, fostering cultural understanding and offering accessible exploration of art and history. We aim to connect global audiences with Europe's rich heritage, promoting education, inspiration, and the sharing of significant stories found within these museums.
